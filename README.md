The Role of a Computer Scientist
Computer scientists are digital architects and problem-solvers. We don't just write code; we design systems that automate decision-making and solve complex tasks efficiently. This work matters because it powers the modern world—from making logistics safer to creating AI that can assist in healthcare and scientific discovery.

My Technical Approach
As a computer scientist, I approach problems through computational thinking:

Decomposition: Breaking the navigation problem into states, actions, and rewards.

Abstraction: Focusing on the mathematical relationship between the agent's current position and the probability of a future reward.

Iterative Refinement: Testing the agent, observing failure patterns in the training data, and adjusting the learning rate or network depth until the agent successfully converges on an optimal path.

Ethical Responsibilities
My responsibilities as a developer extend beyond functional code:

To the Organization: I am responsible for creating maintainable, secure, and well-documented software that adheres to industry standards.

To the End User: I must ensure that AI systems are developed with transparency and safety. In autonomous navigation, this means ensuring the agent's reward system doesn't encourage "risky" behavior that could lead to negative real-world consequences or biased outcomes.
